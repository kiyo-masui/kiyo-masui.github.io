About me

I grew up near Halifax, on the Eastern Shore of Nova Scotia. I left the beauty Nova Scotia to go to Queen's University in Kingston, Ontario. This is where I met my wife Maggie (pictured here a few months after we started dating).

I received my undergrad in Engineering Physics. My undergraduate thesis was supervised by Arthur McDonald and was on sources of radioactively pure argon for dark matter direct detection experiments such as DEAP. I am currently working on my PhD under Ue-Li Pen at the Canadian Institute for Theoretical Astrophysics and the University of Toronto Department of Physics.

When I'm not thinking about about cosmology, I enjoy sailing, hiking, camping, swimming (especially in lakes and oceans) and most other outdoor sports.
